1. Who we are

Seefar Foundation is a Belgian vzw (non-profit association) registered in Brussels (enterprise number: [placeholder]). We help governments and partners respond to irregular migration with evidence-based behaviour-change programmes across 29 countries.

For the purposes of the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and the Belgian Act of 30 July 2018 on the Protection of Natural Persons with regard to the Processing of Personal Data, Seefar Foundation is the data controller for personal data collected through this website.

2. Data we collect

Information you give us directly

  • Contact and enquiry forms — name, email address, organisation, role, and the content of your message when you submit a contact or partnership enquiry.
  • Newsletter sign-ups — name and email address.
  • Job applications — name, contact details, CV, and any supporting information you choose to provide.

Information collected automatically

  • Usage data — pages visited, time spent on each page, referring URL, and general geographic region (country level). We use this only in aggregate and do not build individual profiles.
  • Technical data — browser type, operating system, and screen resolution, used solely for compatibility and performance improvements.
  • Cookies — see Section 4 for full details.

We do not knowingly collect special-category personal data (health, race, political opinion, etc.) through this website.

3. How we use your data

We process personal data only for the following purposes and on the lawful bases indicated:

  • Responding to enquiries — to answer questions you send us (legitimate interests / contract performance).
  • Partnership and donor communications — to follow up on expressions of interest in partnering or funding our work (legitimate interests).
  • Recruitment — to process job applications and contact candidates (legitimate interests / pre-contractual steps).
  • Newsletter — to send updates about our programmes and publications to those who have subscribed (consent).
  • Site analytics — to understand how the website is used and improve it (legitimate interests, with minimal data collection).
  • Legal obligations — to comply with applicable laws and respond to lawful requests from public authorities.

We will not use your data for automated decision-making or profiling that has legal or similarly significant effects on you.

4. Cookies and tracking

We use a small number of first-party cookies. We do not use advertising cookies or third-party tracking pixels.

  • Essential cookies — required for the website to function (e.g. remembering your cookie preference). These cannot be disabled.
  • Analytics cookies — used to measure aggregate page views and navigation patterns. These are only set if you accept cookies via the banner on this site. No data is shared with third parties for advertising purposes.

You can withdraw your cookie consent at any time by clearing your browser cookies or using your browser's privacy settings. Your preference will be reset to the default opt-out state on your next visit.

5. Sharing your data

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal data. We may share data in the following limited circumstances:

  • Service providers — trusted third-party processors (e.g. email delivery, website hosting) who are contractually bound to process data only on our instructions and to appropriate security standards.
  • Legal requirements — where we are required to disclose data by law, court order, or regulatory authority.
  • Organisational transfers — if Seefar Foundation merges with or transfers activities to another non-profit, personal data may be transferred subject to equivalent protections.

All data processors engaged by Seefar Foundation have executed Data Processing Agreements (DPAs) in line with Article 28 GDPR.

6. International transfers

Seefar operates across 29 countries. Where data is transferred outside the European Economic Area (EEA), we rely on one of the following safeguards:

  • An adequacy decision by the European Commission;
  • Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) approved by the European Commission; or
  • Other appropriate safeguards as required by Chapter V GDPR.

You may request a copy of the relevant transfer mechanism by contacting us at the address in Section 11.

7. How long we keep data

  • Contact enquiries — 3 years from last contact, unless an ongoing relationship develops.
  • Newsletter subscriptions — until you unsubscribe or withdraw consent.
  • Job applications (unsuccessful) — 12 months from the closing date of the role, unless you give separate consent for longer retention.
  • Analytics data — aggregated and anonymised within 26 months; raw logs retained for no longer than 14 months.

We review retained data annually and delete or anonymise records that no longer serve their original purpose.

8. Your rights

Under the GDPR, you have the following rights in relation to your personal data:

  • Access — request a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
  • Rectification — ask us to correct inaccurate or incomplete data.
  • Erasure — request deletion of your data where there is no longer a lawful basis for processing it.
  • Restriction — ask us to pause processing in certain circumstances.
  • Portability — receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format where processing is based on consent or contract.
  • Objection — object to processing based on legitimate interests.
  • Withdraw consent — where processing is based on consent, you may withdraw it at any time without affecting the lawfulness of prior processing.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us using the details in Section 11. We will respond within one calendar month. You also have the right to lodge a complaint with the Belgian Data Protection Authority (dataprotectionauthority.be).

9. Children's privacy

This website is not directed at children under 16 years of age. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children. If you believe a child has provided us with personal data without appropriate consent, please contact us and we will delete it promptly.

10. Changes to this policy

We may update this privacy policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices or in applicable law. The "Last updated" date at the top of this page will always reflect the most recent revision. For material changes, we will notify users via a prominent notice on the website or by email where we have your address.

11. Contact us

For any questions about this policy or to exercise your data rights, please contact:

  • Email: privacy@seefar.org
  • Post: Seefar Foundation vzw, [Address placeholder], Brussels, Belgium

We aim to respond to all data-rights requests within 30 days. If your request is complex, we may extend this by a further two months and will notify you accordingly.
